Life Goals

Simply Olives

I grew up in one of many suburban households in Davis, California. Although Davis is known for its cows and bikes, what truly touched me are the black olives. Not many people enjoy black olives, and even fewer can attest to eating a hundred black olives in one sitting.
When I was in elementary school, my father used to bring me my favorite black olive pizza to school every Monday.
As I sit here eating olives now, I realize how little has changed since then. Between his hugs, pats on the back, and smiles of encouragement, my father has always shown me his unwavering care with olives. One day, I met a girl who had just immigrated to the United States. She had not made many friends yet, so I decided to sit next to her and provide her with a piece of my olive pizza that I had that Monday. We have been best friends ever since and have made many great memories together. Olives represent the community I live in, one that provides me with support and teaches me to appreciate and give back. As a child, I ate the olives of my community; as an adult, I will provide them.
